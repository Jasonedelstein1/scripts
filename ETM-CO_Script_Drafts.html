Script Version 3: Emotional Reveal

Reaction-style video featuring Clifton watching student testimonials

Production Note

Required: Parent-filmed videos of students talking about music class. The interviewee would be either the Principal or Karissa. The montage could be shared with Clifton on a phone or screen.

Setup
SCENE: Clifton sits in music classroom (or Principal/Karissa in office)
NARRATOR

Alright, so this is what some of your students had to say about you today. Are you ready?

CLIFTON (OR PRINCIPAL/KARISSA)

Oh man, alright let's do it.

*Montage of parent-filmed students saying nice things about music class/Clifton, cutting back and forth to interview subject getting emotional*

Interview
NARRATOR

What was that like for you?

CLIFTON

That means so much to me. I always want to make an impact, but to hear it from the kids is really sweet. But it's also kind of sad.

NARRATOR

Why is it sad?

CLIFTON

Because this is only my second year here at Centennial, and before that there was no music program.

I have 1st graders that are at the same level as my 5th graders because they missed out on four years of music education.

And as a music teacher, I'm biased, but I don't think education is complete without music.

Call to Action

At too many schools across Colorado, a teacher is missing.

You can bring music back to schools this Colorado Gives Day when you donate to Education Through Music-Colorado.

Production Notes

Key Visual Elements

  • Classroom B-Roll with Music Teacher: Vibrant, colorful classroom with Clifton teaching, students engaged and playing instruments
  • Classroom B-Roll without Music Teacher: Empty, dark, silent classroom with unused instruments collecting dust
  • CTA Visual Contrast: Use before/after classroom footage to emphasize the impact of ETM-CO's work
  • Clifton's Credentials: Performance footage or graphics showing accomplishments
  • Student Joy: Authentic moments of musical discovery and learning
