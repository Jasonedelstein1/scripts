Scripts

Script 1: The Weight We Carry

60-90 SECONDS VOICEOVER REQUIRED

OPENING MONTAGE — ARCHIVAL PHOTOS & VOICEOVER

Voice over: In 1998 I weighed more than four hundred pounds. I couldn't walk up a flight of stairs without stopping. I couldn't fit in a restaurant booth. I couldn't see my feet.

I had three weight-loss surgeries. Two of them nearly killed me. The weight came back every time. And every time it did, I lost a little more of myself.

CUT TO — DON'S HOME, LATE AT NIGHT

Don scrolling through medical weight-loss options on his laptop. Face lit by screen.

CUT TO — DON'S GARAGE OR HOME DEPOT PARKING LOT

Don tries to lift a 50-pound bag of concrete. Struggles. Stops. Shakes his head.

CUT TO — DON WITH A FRIEND, OUTDOORS

Don tries to carry someone piggyback. Takes maybe three steps. Stops, breathing hard. They both laugh, but it's not funny.

CUT TO — DON'S HOME

Don unboxing a weighted vest. Camera close on his face as he realizes the weight of what he's about to do. Loads plates into the vest. Settles for 100 pounds.

CUT TO — MANITOU INCLINE, PARKING LOT, MORNING

Wide shot. Don steps onto a portable scale wearing the 100-pound vest. Numbers settle.

On camera: This is what a hundred pounds feels like. For some people, this is Tuesday.

WIDE ESTABLISHING SHOT — MANITOU INCLINE

The incline rising impossibly steep in front of us.

On camera at base: I'm going to try to climb this wearing a hundred pounds. Because that's what it feels like to wake up every single day when your body is fighting you.

CUT TO — CLIMBING SEQUENCE

Track me up the stairs. Close-ups of feet. Hands gripping rails. Breathing gets harder. Sweat.

On camera, still breathing hard: Everyone says eat better, move more. Sure. But when every step feels like this, starting is the hardest part.

I stop, drop to one knee, unclip the vest. It hits with a dull thud.

On camera: That's why I founded Ascent Trim and Wellness. Because sometimes the hardest part of the climb is taking the first step back to you.

I climb again without the vest. Track me to the summit or cut to summit.

Voice over: You do not have to carry this forever. Let's start getting it off for good, so you can just get back to you.

End card: Ascent Trim and Wellness — Just feel like you again.

Script 3: How It Works

60-75 SECONDS VOICEOVER REQUIRED

OPEN – A-ROLL (Don, direct to camera)

DON: "Most weight-loss advice treats hunger like it's optional. 'Just eat less.' 'Just move more.' 'Just have more discipline.' You've heard it all."

CUTAWAY – SIMPLE B-ROLL / GRAPHIC (humans eating, subtle brain/gut graphic)

DON (V.O.): "But hunger isn't a character flaw. It's a chemical signal. And for a lot of people, that signal is misfiring."

GRAPHIC: FOOD → GUT → HORMONE → BRAIN

DON (V.O.): "When everything's working right, your gut releases a hormone called GLP-1. It tells your brain: 'We've eaten. You can stop now.'

The problem? GLP-1 has a half-life of about two minutes. For many people, that signal fades before it ever reaches the brain."

ANIMATION: GLP-1 SIGNAL FADING BEFORE RECEPTOR

DON (V.O.): "So you're not overeating because you lack willpower. Your brain simply isn't getting the 'I'm full' message."

BACK TO A-ROLL – Don

DON: "Modern medical weight-loss works by activating the same receptor GLP-1 is supposed to hit… and keeping that signal active long enough for your brain to register fullness."

SPLIT VISUAL: "OLD MODEL" VS "NEW MODEL"

DON (V.O.): "It's not about shrinking your stomach. It's not about forcing restriction.

It's about correcting the chemical messaging that drives appetite."

FINAL A-ROLL – Don, steady and clear

DON: "When that signal finally reaches the brain the way it should, your hunger quiets. Your metabolism steadies. And eating starts to feel… normal again."

CTA – A-ROLL + OVERLAY

DON: "If you're ready for weight loss that makes sense — and finally feels sustainable — book your free consultation with Ascent Trim & Wellness."

END CARD / OVERLAY: Logo · URL · "Book your consultation"
