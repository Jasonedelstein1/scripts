Script 1: The Weight We Carry

Subtitle: Tight Cut with "Just Get Back to You"
Estimated Runtime: 60-90 seconds
Primary Message: The hardest part is taking the first step back to yourself

OPENING - ARCHIVAL

Photos of me at my heaviest. Waterskiing. Standing by a tractor-trailer. A mix of those everyday shots I gave you that show the size and strain. The final image lingers on my stomach after surgery, a long line of staples.

Voice over: In 1998 I weighed more than four hundred pounds. Multiple weight loss surgeries nearly killed me, but I was determined to just get back to me.

AT HOME - ONLINE

I scroll ridiculous ways to recreate the weight. Fat suits. Padded vests. Heavy packs. A small laugh. Head shake.

BUDDY SEQUENCE

I hoist a friend for a piggyback up a few steps. His weight flashes on screen.

On camera: Yeah. This is about right. Grimace face mixed with laughter

LOWE'S OR HOME DEPOT

I try to lift a fifty pound bag of concrete. It barely moves.

On camera: So two hundred pounds is four of these. That's not happening.

VEST ARRIVES

I unbox a weighted vest and load plates.

On camera: The goal was two hundred. My back and knees disagree, so we'll call it a hundred. Still plenty to make the point.

BASE OF THE MANITOU INCLINE

Drone pulls back to reveal the climb.

On camera: This is the Manitou Incline in Colorado Springs. Two thousand seven hundred sixty eight stairs straight up. It's beautiful and it's brutal. Back when I was heavy, just crossing a parking lot felt like this.

BEGIN CLIMB

Heavy breathing. Close shots of feet, hands, plates shifting.

On camera, still breathing hard: Everyone says eat better, move more. Sure. But when every step feels like this, starting is the hardest part.

I stop, drop to one knee, unclip the vest. It hits with a dull thud.

On camera: That's why I founded Ascent Trim and Wellness. Because sometimes the hardest part of the climb is taking the first step back to you.

I climb again without the vest. Drone tracks me to the summit or cuts to summit.

Voice over: You do not have to carry this forever. Let's start getting it off for good, so you can just get back to you.

End card: Ascent Trim and Wellness — Just feel like you again.

Script 2: Why I Built This Clinic

Subtitle: Final Working Draft (Clean Narrative Version)
Estimated Runtime: 60-75 seconds
Primary Message: Building trust through vulnerability and authentic motivation

OPENING — CLOSE SHOT, DIRECT TO CAMERA

I didn't build this clinic to make money. I built it because I know exactly what it feels like to wake up and not recognize yourself. To feel tired before the day even starts. To try again and again and never feel like you're winning.

CUT TO — ARCHIVAL PHOTOS OR PERSONAL FOOTAGE

Photos of me at my heaviest. Photos that tell the truth. Diet programs, gym memberships, promises that never lasted.

ON CAMERA: I tried everything. Fad diets, starvation, surgeries that almost took my life. Every time I lost a little weight, I lost a little more of myself.

B-ROLL — REAL WORLD STAND INS (NO CLINIC)

Shots of a park bench at sunrise. A pair of running shoes on a trail. A reflection in a store window. Moments that show what it means to start again.

ON CAMERA: The truth is, people don't need more motivation. They need a way to start that actually works. And for the first time in my life, medicine can help people do that safely.

B-ROLL — SYMBOLIC FOOTAGE

Walking through Garden of the Gods. Talking with someone over coffee. Watching a family hike past on the trail. Natural, everyday scenes that show connection and hope.

ON CAMERA: That's why I built Ascent Trim and Wellness. Because I wish something like this existed when I needed it most. And now it does.

B-ROLL — DRONE SHOT OR TRAIL OVERLOOK

Wind moving through the trees. Quiet. Reflective. Hopeful.

ON CAMERA: If you're ready to start again, we'll do it together. Because the hardest climb isn't up the mountain. It's back to yourself.

END CARD: Ascent Trim and Wellness logo — Just feel like you again.

Script 3: How It Works

Subtitle: Final Script (Don on Camera + VO)
Estimated Runtime: 60-75 seconds
Primary Message: Educating on the science behind modern weight loss treatments

OPEN – A-ROLL (Don, direct to camera)

DON: "Most weight-loss advice treats hunger like it's optional. 'Just eat less.' 'Just move more.' 'Just have more discipline.' You've heard it all."

CUTAWAY – SIMPLE B-ROLL / GRAPHIC (humans eating, subtle brain/gut graphic)

DON (V.O.): "But hunger isn't a character flaw. It's a chemical signal. And for a lot of people, that signal is misfiring."

GRAPHIC: FOOD → GUT → HORMONE → BRAIN

DON (V.O.): "When everything's working right, your gut releases a hormone called GLP-1. It tells your brain: 'We've eaten. You can stop now.'

The problem? GLP-1 has a half-life of about two minutes. For many people, that signal fades before it ever reaches the brain."

ANIMATION: GLP-1 SIGNAL FADING BEFORE RECEPTOR

DON (V.O.): "So you're not overeating because you lack willpower. Your brain simply isn't getting the 'I'm full' message."

BACK TO A-ROLL – Don

DON: "Modern medical weight-loss works by activating the same receptor GLP-1 is supposed to hit… and keeping that signal active long enough for your brain to register fullness."

SPLIT VISUAL: "OLD MODEL" VS "NEW MODEL"

DON (V.O.): "It's not about shrinking your stomach. It's not about forcing restriction.

It's about correcting the chemical messaging that drives appetite."

FINAL A-ROLL – Don, steady and clear

DON: "When that signal finally reaches the brain the way it should, your hunger quiets. Your metabolism steadies. And eating starts to feel… normal again."

CTA – A-ROLL + OVERLAY

DON: "If you're ready for weight loss that makes sense — and finally feels sustainable — book your free consultation with Ascent Trim & Wellness."

END CARD / OVERLAY: Logo · URL · "Book your consultation"
